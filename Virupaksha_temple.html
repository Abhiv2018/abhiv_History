Virupaksha Temple


Virupaksha Temple is located in Hampi in the Ballari district of Karnataka, India.

It is part of the Group of Monuments at Hampi, designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The temple is dedicated to Virupaksha, a form of Shiva.

The temple was built by Lakkan Dandesha, a nayaka (chieftain) under the ruler Deva Raya II of the Vijayanagara Empire
